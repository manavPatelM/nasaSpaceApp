Space

Various Missions: Venus has been explored by several spacecraft, including:

  • Mariner 2: First successful flyby in 1962.
  • Venera Program: Soviet missions that landed probes on Venus.
  • Magellan: Mapped Venus' surface using radar.

Astronomical and Orbital Data

Star Type: G-type (Sun)

Distance from the Sun: 0.72 AU (approximately 108 million kilometers)

Orbital Period: 225 Earth days

Eccentricity: 0.0067 (nearly circular orbit)

Axial Tilt: 177.4 degrees (retrograde rotation)

Rotation Period: ~243 Earth days

Geological Data

Surface Composition: Basaltic rocks, volcanic plains

Crust Thickness: ~30 km (18 miles)

Volcanic Activity: Numerous shield volcanoes and lava flows

Hydrological Data

Liquid Water: None on the surface

Water Vapor: Trace amounts in the atmosphere

Ice Presence: None detected

Environmental Data

Climate: Extremely hot with a dense atmosphere

Surface Temperature: ~465Â°C (hot enough to melt lead)

Albedo: ~0.75 (high reflectivity due to thick clouds)

Atmospheric Data

Atmosphere: Thick and composed mostly of carbon dioxide

Minor Gases: Nitrogen, sulfur dioxide

Surface Pressure: ~92 times that of Earth

Clouds: Sulfuric acid clouds obscure the surface

Wind Speed: Strong winds in the upper atmosphere

Biological and Biochemical Data

Life Potential: Unlikely due to extreme heat and pressure.

Habitability Indicators: No liquid water and a highly acidic environment.

Energy and Resource Data

Energy Sources: Solar energy is abundant above the thick clouds.

Key Elements: Carbon, sulfur, and trace elements dominate Venus' composition.
