Space

Various Missions: Mercury has been studied by several spacecraft, including:

  • Mariner 10: The first spacecraft to fly by Mercury.
  • MESSENGER: Orbited Mercury and provided detailed surface mapping.
  • BepiColombo: Current mission exploring Mercury's surface and environment.

Astronomical and Orbital Data

Star Type: G-type (Sun)

Distance from the Sun: 0.39 AU (approximately 57.9 million kilometers)

Orbital Period: 88 days

Eccentricity: 0.2056 (elliptical orbit)

Axial Tilt: ~0 degrees

Rotation Period: 58.6 days

Geological Data

Surface Composition: Silicate rocks and metals

Crust Thickness: ~35 km (22 miles)

Core: Large metallic core (85% of Mercury's radius)

Seismic Activity: None detected

Hydrological Data

Liquid Water: None

Ice Presence: Water ice in permanently shadowed polar craters

Subsurface Water: None detected

Environmental Data

Climate: Extreme temperature variations; no weather systems

Solar Irradiance: High due to proximity to the Sun

Albedo: ~0.11 (low reflectivity)

Atmospheric Data

Atmosphere: Extremely thin (exosphere)

Major Gases: Oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, potassium

Surface Pressure: ~10^-15 atm

Average Temperature: -173°C at night to 427°C during the day

Radiation: High cosmic radiation, weak magnetic field

Biological and Biochemical Data

Life Potential: None; extreme conditions inhibit life.

Habitability Indicators: No liquid water or nutrients available.

Energy and Resource Data

Energy Sources: Solar energy is abundant due to Mercury's proximity to the Sun.

Key Elements: Silicates, metals such as iron and magnesium.
