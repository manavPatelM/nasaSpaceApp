Space

Various Missions: Neptune has been studied by:

  • Voyager 2: The only spacecraft to visit Neptune, in 1989.
  • Hubble Space Telescope: Observes Neptune's atmosphere and storms.
  • Ground-based Observatories: Monitor Neptune's weather patterns and moons.

Astronomical and Orbital Data

Star Type: G-type (Sun)

Distance from the Sun: 30.1 AU (approximately 4.5 billion kilometers)

Orbital Period: 165 Earth years

Eccentricity: 0.009 (nearly circular orbit)

Axial Tilt: 28.3 degrees

Rotation Period: ~16 hours

Geological Data

Surface Composition: No solid surface; composed of hydrogen, helium, and methane.

Core: Rocky core surrounded by a mantle of water, ammonia, and methane ices.

Internal Heat: Emits more heat than it receives from the Sun.

Hydrological Data

Water Presence: Water exists in the form of ices in the planet's interior.

Atmospheric Methane: Contributes to Neptune's vivid blue color.

Environmental Data

Climate: Dynamic weather systems with the fastest winds in the Solar System.

Average Temperature: ~-214Â°C

Weather: Massive storms and dark spots observed.

Atmospheric Data

Atmosphere: Hydrogen, helium, and methane dominate.

Surface Pressure: Very high in the deeper atmosphere layers.

Cloud Layers: Methane clouds and high-altitude ice clouds.

Biological and Biochemical Data

Life Potential: Extremely low; no known conditions suitable for life.

Habitability Indicators: Lack of liquid water and extreme cold make it inhospitable.

Energy and Resource Data

Energy Sources: Limited solar energy due to distance from the Sun.

Key Elements: Methane, hydrogen, and helium dominate its composition.
