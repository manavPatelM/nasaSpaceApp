Space

Various Missions: Mars has been explored by numerous spacecraft, including:

  • Mariner Program: Early flyby missions.
  • Viking Program: First successful landers.
  • Curiosity Rover: A mobile science laboratory exploring Mars' surface.
  • Perseverance Rover: Searching for signs of past microbial life and collecting samples.

Astronomical and Orbital Data

Star Type: G-type (Sun)

Distance from the Sun: 1.52 AU (approximately 227.9 million kilometers)

Orbital Period: 687 days

Eccentricity: 0.0934 (more elliptical than Earth's orbit)

Axial Tilt: 25.2 degrees

Rotation Period: 24.6 hours

Geological Data

Surface Composition: Basaltic rocks, iron oxide (responsible for its red color)

Crust Thickness: ~50 km (31 miles)

Core: Likely a mix of iron, nickel, and sulfur

Seismic Activity: Detected by Mars InSight lander

Hydrological Data

Liquid Water: None detected on the surface

Ice Presence: Substantial water ice at poles and beneath the surface

Subsurface Water: Potential briny water detected in specific regions

Environmental Data

Climate: Thin atmosphere leads to extreme temperature variations

Solar Irradiance: Weaker than Earth's due to distance from the Sun

Albedo: ~0.15 (moderate reflectivity)

Atmospheric Data

Atmosphere: Thin, primarily carbon dioxide

Major Gases: 95.3% CO2, 2.7% nitrogen, 1.6% argon

Surface Pressure: ~0.006 atm

Average Temperature: -63°C, with extremes from -143°C to 20°C

Radiation: High cosmic radiation, no significant magnetic field

Biological and Biochemical Data

Life Potential: No confirmed life, though microbial life is possible

Habitability Indicators: Past presence of liquid water; potential subsurface brines

Energy and Resource Data

Energy Sources: Solar energy available; potential geothermal sources

Key Elements: Iron, sulfur, and trace amounts of essential elements
