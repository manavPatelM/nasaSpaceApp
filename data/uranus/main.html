Space

Various Missions: Uranus has been studied by:

  • Voyager 2: The only spacecraft to visit Uranus, in 1986.
  • Hubble Space Telescope: Observed Uranus' rings and atmosphere.
  • Ground-based Observatories: Provide ongoing data on Uranus' weather and rotation.

Astronomical and Orbital Data

Star Type: G-type (Sun)

Distance from the Sun: 19.8 AU (approximately 2.9 billion kilometers)

Orbital Period: 84 Earth years

Eccentricity: 0.047 (slightly elliptical orbit)

Axial Tilt: 97.8 degrees (rotates on its side)

Rotation Period: ~17 hours 14 minutes

Geological Data

Surface Composition: No solid surface; composed of hydrogen, helium, and methane.

Core: Rocky core surrounded by icy layers.

Internal Heat: Very low heat emission compared to other gas giants.

Hydrological Data

Water Presence: Water exists in the form of ice in the planet's interior.

Atmospheric Methane: Gives Uranus its blue-green color.

Environmental Data

Climate: Coldest planetary atmosphere in the Solar System.

Average Temperature: ~-224Â°C

Weather: Methane clouds and weak wind patterns.

Atmospheric Data

Atmosphere: Hydrogen, helium, and methane dominate.

Surface Pressure: Higher than Earth's due to dense atmosphere.

Cloud Layers: Ammonia and methane clouds observed.

Biological and Biochemical Data

Life Potential: Extremely low; no known conditions suitable for life.

Habitability Indicators: Lack of liquid water and extreme cold make it inhospitable.

Energy and Resource Data

Energy Sources: Limited solar energy due to distance from the Sun.

Key Elements: Methane, hydrogen, and helium dominate its composition.
